Dashboard Design Tips

Use Clear Titles

Make your dashboard sections instantly recognizable with descriptive headings. Use action words and be specific about the metrics being displayed. For example, "Monthly Revenue Growth" is better than just "Revenue".

Color-code KPIs

Use consistent colors to help viewers quickly identify different metrics and their status. Green for positive trends, red for alerts, and yellow for warnings. Keep your color scheme consistent across all visualizations for better comprehension.

Keep it Clean

Don't overload your dashboard. Focus on the most important metrics for better clarity. Use white space effectively and group related metrics together. Consider using tabs or drill-downs for detailed views.

Mobile-First Design

Ensure your dashboard is responsive and works well on all devices. Prioritize the most important metrics for mobile view and consider using stacked layouts for smaller screens.

Time Period Controls

Include easy-to-use date range selectors and comparison periods. Allow users to switch between different time frames (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly) for better trend analysis.

Export Options

Provide options to export data and visualizations in various formats (PDF, Excel, PNG). Include print-friendly layouts for better reporting capabilities.
